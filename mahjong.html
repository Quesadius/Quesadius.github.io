Winning Hands

Complete any one of these 14-tile hands to win. ABC mark three different suits (bams / craks / dots) — you choose which. Jokers are wild in pungs (3), kongs (4) and quints (5), but never in a pair or single. Flowers are interchangeable. A concealed hand must have no exposed melds.

How to play

Goal. Be the first to arrange your tiles into one of the listed winning hands.

Deal. The host (East) starts with 14 tiles, the guest with 13, drawn from a shared 152-tile wall. East discards first.

Your turn. When it's your turn you either draw the next wall tile, or claim the tile your opponent just discarded to form a Pung (3 of a kind) or Kong (4 of a kind), exposing that meld face-up. Then you discard one tile.

Jokers are wild inside a pung, kong, or quint — never in a pair or a single. Flowers are all interchangeable. The white dragon doubles as "soap".

Winning. Complete a listed hand and press Mahjong — either after drawing/claiming on your own turn, or by claiming the exact discard that finishes your hand. A jokerless win scores double. If the wall runs out, the hand is a draw.

This is a two-player adaptation in the spirit of "Siamese" Mahjong, with an original hand set. It is not affiliated with the NMJL or Siamese Mah Jongg®.
